# Mine Safety Stories

By Brian Hamman
*IRE and NICAR*

In the days and weeks after the Sago Mine disaster in West Virginia, journalists used their computer-assisted reporting skills to dig more deeply into stories about mine safety.

The U.S. Mine Safety and Health Administration (MSHA) offers more than 20 years of accident, inspection, and violation data on its Web site that journalists can use to add context to a quick-hit story or as the basis of an investigation.

When they set out to study the violations at Colorado's mines, Katy Human and Jeff Roberts of *The Denver Post* found they had to begin with a more basic question: "Our first question was, do we have underground coal mines in Colorado?" Human says.

To get an overview of mining in Colorado, Human and Roberts turned to the data retrieval system provided by MSHA, available at *www.msha.gov/drs/drshome.htm.*
