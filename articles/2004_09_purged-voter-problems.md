# Purged voter problems

By Chris Davis and Matthew Doig
*Sarasota Herald-Tribune*

Our analysis of a controversial database that Florida election officials were using to purge felons from the voter rolls exposed a fatal flaw that led to the list's demise. The analysis was simple, though the story was anything but ordinary.

We imported the state's data into Microsoft Excel. With the help of a pivot table, we found how many of the 48,000 people on the list were white, black or Hispanic. Excel's pivot table function allows users to create cross-tabs to categorize and summarize data.

The check was a basic step any journalist should take after he or she first gets new data.

Immediately, the problem appeared in the results – 24,000 of the potential felons were white, 22,000 were black, and 61 were Hispanic. We knew that Hispanics in Florida make up a key Republican voting bloc.
