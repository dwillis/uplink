# Politics and elections: An evolving story

By David Herzog, *NICAR and Missouri School of Journalism*

Covering elections and politics is an ongoing enterprise. The November presidential election is over and the crush of resulting stories has lightened. Still, it's good to remember that some of the best stories coming out of the elections are the ones developing now, the ones you can do any time of the year to examine subjects that are key to understanding our democracy: political power and voter registration, to name just two.

In the most recent election season, journalists from coast to coast fired up their databases and discovered interesting stories about voters registered in more than one locale, political nonprofit contributions and problems with felon-voter purge lists – all great stories. From the stories reported so far, it's clear that even more are waiting to be uncovered.

This issue of *Uplink*, starting with the front page article by IRE and NICAR's Andrea Lorenz, presents resources for journalists who want to stay on the trail through the year ahead.
