# Images boost GIS work

By Holly Hacker
*IRE and NICAR*

Most GIS-savvy journalists are familiar with shapefiles, points and polygons. They can place dots on a map to show crimes in their neighborhood, or code Census tracts to identify neighborhoods that gained or lost the most residents.

But for journalists who use a geographic information system, the next spatial frontier to conquer is remote sensing, according to experts in the business.

The *St. Louis Post-Dispatch* recently discovered the dynamic stories that can be told by combining satellite images with other information in a GIS. The newspaper hired researchers in Minnesota to study development on the Upper Mississippi River Basin since the flood of 1993.
