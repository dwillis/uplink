# Year after attacks files still guarded

By Jennifer LaFleur
*Reporters Committee for Freedom of the Press*

After Congress passed the 1996 amendments to the Freedom of Information Act, government agencies began putting more data and documents on their Web sites.

But that trend reversed after Sept. 11, 2001, when several federal agencies moved to take down maps, databases and entire Web sites from the public domain citing security reasons. Journalists no longer could get to the data they had used before in news stories.

In particular the door was shut to environmental data, transportation maps, dam locations and other databases frequently used by reporters, community groups and citizens.
