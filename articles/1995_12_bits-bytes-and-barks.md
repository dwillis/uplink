# Bits, Bytes and Barks

### A New Patent Site with New Problems

The new U.S. Patent and Trademark Information site is much prettier than its Internet Multicasting Service predecessor, and it professes to include patents back to 1985 where IMS was limited to 1994 and 1995. The interface supports everything from simple point-and-click to full Boolean language searching by field, and is easy to use.

One problem of the IMS site that shows up again here is that you may only search one year at a time. A warning: a quick search for inventors in Nashville (a search I performed often at the IMS site) turned up just 18 patents, much less than the 50-60 I was used to experiencing. And the latest of those patents was from June. This could have been a bug in the system, which was still being worked on when I logged on (Nov. 13). But beware of assuming that any search is pulling up "all" patents on that subject in the given year.

— Heather Newman, The Tennessean; send e-mail to hnewman@tennessean.com
